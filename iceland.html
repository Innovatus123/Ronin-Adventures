Destination Overview

Fire, Ice,
and No Crowds.

Iceland sits on the Mid-Atlantic Ridge — the geological seam where two tectonic plates pull apart at 2.5 centimeters per year. The result is an island of active volcanoes, geysers, black-sand beaches, and glaciers that cover 10% of the landmass. The Northern Lights are a side effect.

Ronin Adventures positions you at the edge of all of it: a private glacier hike on Vatnajökull, Europe's largest ice cap; a helicopter landing inside a dormant volcano crater; and a geothermal pool in the Westfjords with no other human being within 20 kilometers.

Best Time to Visit

Two distinct seasons

Northern Lights: November–March (darkest nights, best aurora probability). Midnight Sun: May–August (24-hour daylight, all roads open). We design for both.

Duration

7 – 12 days

The Ring Road circuit covers 1,332km and takes 7 days minimum at a meaningful pace. Add Westfjords or Highlands for 10+ day itineraries.

Experience Level

Moderate — some physical activity

Glacier hikes require crampons and basic fitness. All activities are fully guided. Luxury accommodation available regardless of activity level.

Entry Requirements

Schengen visa-free for most passports

US, UK, and most Western passports enter visa-free. Weather dictates itinerary flexibility — we build contingency routes into every plan.

What We Include

Iceland
Highlights

🌋

Volcano Crater Helicopter

Land inside the Thrihnukagigur volcano magma chamber — the only place on Earth where you can descend into a dormant volcano via cable elevator. Private helicopter from Reykjavik.

🧊

Vatnajökull Glacier Hike

Europe's largest glacier — private crampons-equipped hike with a glaciologist guide who's mapped its recession for 20 years. Ice caves only accessible November–March.

🌌

Northern Lights Positioning

We use a dedicated aurora forecast meteorologist and position you in the Westfjords or Highlands, away from light pollution, with a 72-hour advance prediction system.

♨️

Remote Geothermal Pools

Private access to natural hot springs in the Westfjords and Landmannalaugar — not the Blue Lagoon. Soaking in 38°C water while snow falls, with no other visitors visible.

🐋

Whale Watching, Húsavík

Húsavík is the whale-watching capital of Europe — humpbacks, minkes, and blue whales in the Skjálfandi Bay. Private boat charter with a marine biologist guide.

🏔️

Highlands & Landmannalaugar

The Icelandic interior — accessible only in summer via 4WD on F-roads. Rhyolite mountains in volcanic reds, yellows, and greens. Hikeable and utterly alien.

Sample Journey

10 Days in Iceland.

01

Reykjavik Arrival

Private transfer from Keflavik International. Reykjavik hotel, dinner at a Nordic tasting menu restaurant, and evening aurora check with our forecast team.

02

Golden Circle & Volcano

Private guided Golden Circle — Þingvellir (where tectonic plates are visible on the surface), Geysir, and Gullfoss waterfall. Afternoon helicopter to the volcano crater.

03–04

South Coast

Seljalandsfoss (walk behind the waterfall), Skógafoss, Reynisfjara black-sand beach, and Jökulsárlón glacier lagoon. Two nights at a glass-ceiling lodge facing the aurora corridor.

05–06

Vatnajökull Glacier

Private glaciologist guide for a 2-day ice exploration — glacier hike, ice cave (seasonal), and snowmobile across the ice cap with views of five volcanic peaks.

07–08

Eastfjords & Highlands

Private 4WD convoy through the Eastfjords and into the Highlands via F-roads. Overnight at a remote mountain hut. Landmannalaugar volcanic landscape on Day 8.

09

Westfjords

Charter flight to the Westfjords — Iceland's least-visited region. Natural geothermal pool with no other visitors. Látrabjarg cliff, Europe's largest seabird colony.

10

Reykjavik Departure

Return to Reykjavik. Morning at the Hallgrímskirkja tower. Afternoon departure from Keflavik.
