Destination Overview

Two Oceans,
One Mountain.

Cape Town was founded in 1652 as a Dutch East India Company provisioning station and has been one of the world's most strategically positioned cities ever since. It sits 50 kilometers from the Cape of Good Hope — the point where the Atlantic and Indian Oceans merge — with a flat-topped mountain rising 1,086 meters directly above the city center.

Ronin Adventures designs Cape Town itineraries that move across the city's remarkable range: a private hike up Table Mountain's eastern face before the cable car opens, a great white shark cage dive at Dyer Island, and a wine tasting at a Stellenbosch estate that pre-dates the Cape Colony by 50 years.

Best Time to Visit

October – April (Summer)

Cape Town's summer delivers clear skies, warm temperatures, and the wine harvest. Winter (May–September) is wet and cold but offers whale watching in Hermanus and empty wine estates.

Duration

7 – 12 days

3 days in Cape Town, 2 days wine route, 1 day shark diving, Cape Peninsula day trip, optional Garden Route extension (3 days) or Kruger safari connection.

Experience Level

All levels

Shark cage diving requires no experience (not even swimming — cages are at surface level). Table Mountain hike is moderate. Wine route is gentle. We calibrate everything.

Entry Requirements

Visa-free for most Western passports

US, EU, and UK passports enter South Africa visa-free for 90 days. We coordinate all shark dive permits, Table Mountain permits, and wine estate access.

What We Include

Cape Town
Highlights

🏔️

Table Mountain Private Ascent

The eastern face trail before 6 AM — no cable car, no crowds, a 3-hour climb arriving at the flat top as the city lights below begin to go out.

🦈

Great White Shark Cage Dive

Dyer Island off Gansbaai has the highest concentration of great white sharks on Earth. A steel cage at surface level — no diving certification required.

🍷

Cape Winelands Private Route

Stellenbosch, Franschhoek, and Paarl wine estates in a private vehicle with a certified Cape sommelier. Cellar tours, barrel tastings, and a farm-to-table lunch at an estate.

🐋

Whale Watching, Hermanus

Southern right whales calve in Walker Bay June–November — the world's best land-based whale watching. Private boat permits access the calving grounds more closely.

🌊

Cape Point & Two Oceans

Private vehicle to the Cape of Good Hope — the confluence of the Atlantic and Indian Oceans. Boulder's Beach African penguin colony on the return leg.

🌆

Robben Island & History

The maximum security island prison where Nelson Mandela was held for 18 years. Private early ferry access and a guide who was personally incarcerated here.

Sample Journey

9 Days at the Cape.

01

Cape Town Arrival

Private transfer to a V&A Waterfront luxury hotel. Table Mountain backdrop orientation. Welcome dinner at a rooftop restaurant above the working harbour.

02

Table Mountain & Bo-Kaap

Pre-dawn departure for the eastern face summit hike. Arrive at the top before the cable car opens. Afternoon walk through Bo-Kaap — Cape Malay Quarter, painted houses, spice kitchen.

03

Cape Winelands

Private vehicle to Stellenbosch and Franschhoek. Three estate visits with a certified sommelier — reserve tastings, a Huguenot heritage walk, and lunch at a winery dating to 1694.

04

Shark Cage Dive

Early departure to Gansbaai (2 hrs). Great white shark cage dive at Dyer Island. Optional afternoon at Hermanus for whale watching (in season).

05

Cape Peninsula

Private vehicle to the Cape Point National Park — Chapman's Peak Drive, Cape of Good Hope, penguin colony at Boulders Beach. Sunset cocktails at the Cape of Two Oceans.

06

Robben Island & History

Private early ferry to Robben Island. Private guided tour with an ex-political prisoner. Afternoon at the Cape Town Holocaust Museum and District Six Museum.

07–09

Kruger Safari Connection

Domestic flight to Kruger National Park. 3 days of private-vehicle game drives in the north — greater density of wildlife, fewer vehicles, and guides who know specific animal territories.
