Destination Overview

Hills That
Built Civilization.

Tuscany produced the Renaissance. The Medici banking dynasty funded Leonardo, Michelangelo, Botticelli, and Galileo from Florence while simultaneously controlling the wine estates, trade routes, and architecture of the surrounding region. The hills are older than that — the Etruscans were pressing wine in the Chianti zone 2,700 years ago.

Ronin Adventures designs Tuscany itineraries around the estate and the artist: private access to Medici villa collections before public opening, truffle hunting at dawn with a third-generation tartufaio and his dog, and a wine estate dinner for six with the winemaker explaining a 1997 Brunello di Montalcino.

Best Time to Visit

April – June and September – October

Harvest season (September–October) is the definitive Tuscan experience — private cellar access, grape picking, and wine events. Spring delivers wildflower landscapes. Avoid August peak heat and crowds.

Duration

7 – 10 days

Florence (3 days), Chianti wine country (2 days), Montalcino (2 days), Val d'Orcia (2 days). Optional coastal extension at Forte dei Marmi or the Maremma.

Experience Level

All levels — gentle terrain

Tuscany is wine country — the physical demands are minimal. We design walking tours in hill towns, private vehicle access, and cycling routes at your pace.

Entry Requirements

Schengen visa-free for most passports

US, UK, and most Western passports enter Italy visa-free for 90 days. We manage all Uffizi access, estate bookings, and chef's table reservations.

What We Include

Tuscany
Highlights

🎨

Uffizi Private Access

Florence's Uffizi Gallery contains 93 rooms of Renaissance masterworks — including Botticelli's Birth of Venus and Primavera. We access it before public hours with a private art historian.

🍄

Truffle Hunt at Dawn

A pre-dawn departure into the Crete Senesi with a third-generation tartufaio and his trained lagotto dogs. White truffles (November) or black (year-round). Lunch using that morning's harvest.

🍷

Brunello Estate Access

Montalcino produces what many consider Italy's greatest red wine. Private barrel room access at a top-10 estate, vertical tasting with the winemaker, and cellar-door pricing.

🏡

Private Villa Stay

An agriturismo or private villa estate with a resident chef — complete with an orchard, olive press, and vegetable garden that supplies the kitchen. No other guests.

🌄

Val d'Orcia Landscape

UNESCO-listed rolling hills, cypress-lined roads, and medieval hilltop towns — exactly as depicted in Renaissance paintings, because this is the landscape those paintings depicted.

🍝

Florentine Culinary Access

A Michelin kitchen stage with a Florentine chef, a private market walk at Sant'Ambrogio before it opens, and a bistecca alla fiorentina dinner at a restaurant that's been open since 1869.

Sample Journey

9 Days in Tuscany.

01

Florence Arrival

Private transfer from Florence Airport or Santa Maria Novella station. Afternoon walk across the Ponte Vecchio and through the Oltrarno. Welcome dinner at a family trattoria that doesn't appear in guidebooks.

02

Uffizi & Medici Florence

Pre-opening Uffizi access with a private art historian — 2 hours before the public enters. Medici Chapel, Brunelleschi's Dome climb, and the Accademia (Michelangelo's David). Evening food walk in the San Lorenzo market district.

03

Fiesole & Hill Towns

Drive to Fiesole — the Etruscan hill town above Florence with panoramic city views. Afternoon to San Gimignano — the medieval Manhattan of Tuscany, 14 towers visible from the highway.

04

Chianti Wine Route

Private vehicle through the Chianti Classico zone — Greve, Castellina, Radda. Three estate visits with a certified WSET sommelier. Harvest participation if visiting September–October.

05

Truffle Hunt & Montalcino

Pre-dawn truffle hunt in the Crete Senesi. Truffle-based lunch at the tartufaio's farmhouse. Afternoon drive to Montalcino — tasting at a Brunello estate by private appointment.

06

Val d'Orcia

Drive through the Val d'Orcia UNESCO landscape — Pienza (Renaissance ideal city), Montepulciano, and Bagno Vignoni (thermal baths in a medieval square). Overnight in a private agriturismo.

07–08

Siena & Private Villa

Siena Cathedral (Duomo) private access — the inlaid marble floor only uncovered September–October. Palazzo Pubblico and Piazza del Campo. Day 8: at leisure at the villa estate — wine, olive oil, and cooking class.

09

Return to Florence & Departure

Morning drive back through Chianti. Farewell espresso at the bar that originated the form. Florence Airport departure.
