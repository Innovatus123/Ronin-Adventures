Destination Overview

The Most
Beautiful Walls.

Cartagena was founded in 1533 and spent 200 years as the most important port of the Spanish Empire in the Americas — the city through which all South American gold and silver flowed toward Spain. The 11-kilometer colonial wall that surrounds the old city was built to protect it, and it did: Cartagena was never successfully sacked.

Ronin Adventures designs Cartagena itineraries that use the city as a base for Colombia's Caribbean coast: private sailing through the Rosario Islands, diving the coral walls at the Corales del Rosario National Park, and access to the old city's private courtyards and rooftop dining through connections that no booking platform carries.

Best Time to Visit

December – April (Dry Season)

The Caribbean dry season delivers clear skies and calm seas. Cartagena's Literary Festival (January) and Hay Festival (January) attract international cultural events. May–November is wetter but less crowded.

Duration

5 – 8 days

3 days in the old city, 2 days Rosario Islands sailing, optional Tayrona National Park extension (2 days) or Medellín city connection (2 days).

Experience Level

All levels

No physical demands for the city circuit. Diving requires certification (or resort courses available). Sailing is fully crewed.

Entry Requirements

No visa for most Western passports

US, EU, UK, and most passports enter Colombia visa-free for 90 days. We manage all sailing permits, dive operator bookings, and private courtyard access.

What We Include

Cartagena
Highlights

🏰

Old City Private Walking Tour

An architectural historian who has mapped every courtyard in the walled city — private early morning walk before the heat and before other visitors arrive.

⛵

Rosario Islands Sailing Charter

A private sailboat through the Colombian Caribbean archipelago — turquoise water, white sand, and coral visible from the deck. Anchored lunch on a private island.

🤿

Coral Wall Diving

The Corales del Rosario Marine National Park has Caribbean coral walls at 15–40m depth — nurse sharks, sea turtles, and spotted eagle rays. PADI certification or resort dive available.

🌺

Palenque Village Access

San Basilio de Palenque — the first free African town in the Americas, founded in 1603 by escaped slaves. A UNESCO-listed cultural landscape with its own language, Palenquero.

🍽️

Cartagena Culinary Access

Colombia's culinary capital — ceviche de camarones, patacones, and a rooftop restaurant scene that received its first Michelin recognition in 2023. We access tables that require local connections.

🏖️

Tayrona National Park

A 150-km drive east — a protected jungle-backed Caribbean beach that requires a 3-hour hike to reach and limits daily visitors to 200. We time your access for dawn.

Sample Journey

7 Days in Colombia.

01

Cartagena Arrival

Private transfer to a converted colonial mansion in the old city. Rooftop cocktails above the cathedral at dusk. Welcome dinner at a courtyard restaurant that requires weeks of advance booking.

02

Old City Walk & History

Pre-dawn walk through the Getsemaní neighborhood with an architectural historian. Colonial churches, slave trade history, and the private courtyards behind locked wooden doors.

03

Rosario Islands Sailing

Board your private sailboat at the city harbor. Full day in the archipelago — snorkeling on a reef, anchored lunch on a private island, return at sunset with the city walls lit orange.

04

Diving & Palenque

Morning dive at the Corales del Rosario coral wall. Afternoon private vehicle to San Basilio de Palenque — meet a Palenquero language teacher and a traditional healer.

05

Tayrona National Park

Pre-dawn drive to the park entrance. 3-hour jungle and beach hike to Cabo San Juan de la Guía. Limited 200-person daily entry — we secure permits months ahead. Return by dusk.

06–07

Medellín Extension or Leisured City Day

Option A: 1-hour flight to Medellín — Pablo Escobar's transformation city, now Latin America's most innovative urban design case study. Option B: Leisure day in Cartagena — cooking class, spa, and a private sunset sail.
