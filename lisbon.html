Destination Overview

Where Europe
Ends.

Lisbon was the capital of the world's first global empire — the city from which Vasco da Gama departed to reach India in 1498, and from which explorers mapped half the known world over the following century. The Alfama district and the Moorish castle on the hill above it predate the Portuguese age of discovery by 500 years.

Ronin Adventures designs Lisbon itineraries built around access and appetite: a private fado concert in a 14th-century monastery, a wine route through the Douro Valley in a private vehicle with a sommelier, and a chef's table dinner that required a six-month waiting list.

Best Time to Visit

March – May and September – November

Spring and autumn deliver 18–24°C temperatures with low crowds. Summer is warm but crowded. December–February is mild (14°C) and almost tourist-free.

Duration

5 – 8 days

3 days in Lisbon proper, plus day trips to Sintra (UNESCO palace village), Setúbal coast, and optional 2-night Douro Valley wine extension.

Experience Level

All levels — the city is hilly

Lisbon's seven hills require comfortable shoes. We arrange private vehicle access to hilltop viewpoints so the walk is always optional.

Entry Requirements

Schengen visa-free for most passports

US, UK, and most Western passports enter Portugal visa-free for 90 days. We manage all restaurant reservations, palace access, and wine region permits.

What We Include

Lisbon
Highlights

🏰

Sintra Palace Circuit

UNESCO World Heritage village 30km from Lisbon — the Moorish Castle, Pena Palace in polychrome neoromantic excess, and Quinta da Regaleira with its underground initiation well. Private early entry.

🍷

Douro Valley Wine Route

The world's oldest demarcated wine region — terraced vineyards above the Douro River at grades that no machine can harvest. Private Quinta tastings with a resident oenologist.

🎵

Private Fado Session

Fado is Portugal's UNESCO-listed musical soul — a tradition of longing sung in small caves in the Alfama. We arrange private performances in venues that don't accept public reservations.

🍽️

Chef's Table Access

Lisbon has 8 Michelin stars, including restaurants that require months of advance planning. We secure reservations that aren't publicly bookable.

⛵

Tagus & Atlantic Sailing

Private sailing yacht from the Tagus estuary into the Atlantic — the same waters da Gama's fleet departed through in 1498. Sunset return under the 25 de Abril bridge.

🏺

Alfama District Private Walk

The oldest quarter of Lisbon, built on a Moorish grid that predates the Portuguese. Private morning walk with an architectural historian — before the tram tourists arrive.

Sample Journey

7 Days in Portugal.

01

Lisbon Arrival

Private transfer from Humberto Delgado Airport. Alfama viewpoint at dusk. Welcome dinner at a restaurant in a 16th-century monastery cloister.

02

Alfama & Moorish Lisbon

Private morning walk through the Alfama with an architectural historian — Miradouro da Graça, São Jorge Castle, and the streets unchanged since the 1755 earthquake reconstruction.

03

Sintra Day Trip

Private car to Sintra. Early entry to the Moorish Castle before public opening. Pena Palace and Quinta da Regaleira with a private guide. Return to Lisbon for a fado evening in the Alfama.

04

Belém & Maritime History

The Belém Tower and the Jerónimos Monastery — UNESCO landmarks built on Portuguese spice trade profits. Private curator-led access to the archaeology wing before public hours.

05

Setúbal Coast

Private vehicle south to the Serra da Arrábida Natural Park — limestone cliffs and turquoise water, completely undeveloped. Boat tour of the marine reserve. Seafood lunch at a cliff-edge restaurant.

06–07

Douro Valley

Private car to Porto (3 hrs). Overnight in a converted 18th-century quinta on the Douro River. Full day of private winery visits — reserve tastings, cellar tours, and lunch with the winemaker.
